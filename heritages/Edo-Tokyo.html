Edo-Tokyo Museum






Exhibits (Highlights):
Life-sized Nihonbashi Bridge Replica: This iconic bridge marked the entrance to Edo (old Tokyo) during the Edo period. Visitors can experience the scale and architectural details of this historic landmark.




Scale Model Cityscapes: Detailed models depict Edo, Tokyo, and their evolution across various eras:
1590 (late 16th century - Edo period beginning)
1868 (Meiji Restoration marking the shift to a modern state)
1923 (Great Kanto Earthquake devastation)
1964 (Post-WWII reconstruction and modernization) These models allow visitors to visually grasp the dramatic changes and urban growth over centuries.
Nakamuraza Theatre Replica: A meticulously recreated kabuki theatre from the Edo period. Kabuki is a traditional Japanese theatrical form known for its elaborate costumes, dramatic performances, and stylized movements. This replica offers a glimpse into the world of Edo entertainment.




Additional Exhibits:



Edo Period Daily Life: Explore how people lived, worked, and played during this time, with displays on clothing, food, housing, and occupations.
Great Kanto Earthquake: Learn about the devastating earthquake of 1923, its impact on the city, and the subsequent reconstruction efforts.
Post-WWII Tokyo: Discover the city's remarkable recovery and rise as a global center in the wake of World War II.
